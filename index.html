General Info

The Roles

Team Devs Product Owner Scrum Master
Includes Dev, QE, PM, PMO The people that make the user story a reality. This includes QE. Person responsible for the direction of the product Facilitator of the process
Owns backlog priority Information radiator
Usually responsible for writing user stories

Obstacles are Not Roadblocks

Product owner is not always a single person
A User Story Owner isn’t necessarily a Product person. An Engineer might be the one to represent a user story during grooming. An example of this might be a story created by Eng for paying down some technical debt.
Scrum Master may not always be a PMO
Being a geographically dispersed team, it may not always be possible to have PMO act as SM. Another person may fill the role and work closely with PMO in those cases.
Devs are versatile
Devs may need to do activity that isn’t coding such as work on requirements or docs. That’s OK and can still have stories for the sprints.
Apart but together
Agile was built around co-location. A high level of communication and togetherness are keys to success. Use tools as much as possible to better communicate and foster a sense of teamwork. The daily stand-up is great for togetherness. Email as a last option.

Meetings

In a nutshell
Sprint Planning
  • Duration: 1-4 hours on the first day of the sprint
  • Mandatory: Devs, Scrum Master
  • Optional: Product Owner should be “on call” to answer questions
Daily Stand-up
  • Duration: 15 mins max each day of the sprint
  • Mandatory: Devs, Scrum Master
  • Optional: Product Owner
Backlog Refinement
  • Duration: 1 hour twice a sprint or as needed
  • Mandatory: Product Owner
  • Optional: Scrum Master
Backlog Grooming
  • Duration: 1 hour twice a sprint
  • Mandatory: Devs, Scrum Master and Product Owner
Retrospective
  • Duration: 1 hour on the last day of the sprint
  • Mandatory: Devs, Scrum Master
  • Optional: Product Owner is optional but highly encouraged to be there

Meetings

Explained

Sprint Planning

Goal Agree on work to be completed during the sprint and create a plan to achieve the goal by creating tasks that will meet the acceptance criteria for each story in the sprint.
Method Team chooses which stories they will commit to for the sprint by selecting from the top of the product backlog. Ideally stories are broken down into tasks and sized in hours. Tasking and sizing allows the team to gut-check the amount of work they took on from the product backlog.
Artifact The team should produce a sprint goal and a sprint backlog by the end of sprint planning.

Daily Stand-up

Goal By focusing on what each person accomplished yesterday and will accomplish today, the team gains an excellent understanding of what work has been done and what work remains. The daily scrum meeting is not a status update meeting in which a boss is collecting information about who is behind schedule. Rather, it is a meeting in which team members make commitments to each other.
Method Devs answer to each other the following three questions:
  • What did you do yesterday?
  • What will you do today?
  • Are you blocked by any impediments?
Artifact Clear understanding of the work for the day by the team.

Backlog Prep

Goal User story rank is set by Product. Stories at the top of the backlog are ready to be groomed by the team by having a good title, description and acceptance criteria.
Method Product reviews new stories and defects and ranks them in the product backlog. They edit stories for clarity and create new stories as needed.
Artifact Product backlog is ready for grooming.

Backlog Grooming

Goal Review product backlog items and have a meeting of the minds between Product Owner and team. Update stories as needed. Stories are sized.
Method Scrum Master steps through unsized stories starting with the highest ranked item in the product backlog. Team reviews and sizes story.
Artifact Stories are sized using story points.

Retrospective

Goal Reflect on the sprint to allow the team to inspect and adapt.
Method Retrospectives are facilitated by the Scrum Master and can vary based on the personal style of the Scrum Master. The following items are usually touched on during the retro:
  • Things the team wants to start doing, stop doing and/or continue doing.
  • What went well, what didn’t go so well, things to try and escalations.
Artifact Happy, healthy, high-performing team.

Sprint Demo

What is a sprint demo?
Each team should end the sprint with a demonstration of the working software they produced in the sprint. This is a meeting driven by the team themselves and should last less than an hour. It is an informal, festive occasion to celebrate the great work that the team did.
Only fully finished stories that meet all of the acceptance criteria should be shown at the demo.
What a sprint demo is not
This is not a meeting for the Product Owner to accept the story (this will have already occurred during the sprint). It also isn’t a debate about the merit of a story.
Feedback is always welcome but best handled offline with the Product Owner where a follow-on story can be created for future sprints.
Challenges
This is a meeting that is most impactful if the majority of participants are there in-person. Having distributed teams and/or audiences makes having a great demo more challenging but is still worth having in most cases. It should be a team decision on whether-or-not to have a public demo but it is highly encouraged.

Go!!

Day 1 - Sprint Planning

Goal Agree on work to be completed during the sprint and create a plan to achieve the goal by creating tasks that will meet the acceptance criteria for each story in the sprint
Team Planning for the sprint by reviewing backlog, sizing and tasking
Devs
  • Take in work from the product backlog to be worked on this sprint
  • Task out the user stories and assign hours to the tasks
Product Owner Meeting with the team as requested and needed by the team
Scrum Master
  • Facilitating the planning and tasking meeting
  • Working with team on estimated sprint velocity and team capacity
  • Update Jira

Day 2 - Development

Goal Begin Development
Team Daily Standup
Devs Coding, creating test cases and design
Product Owner Re-rank backlog after changes made during planning
Scrum Master Review sprint plan in tool to ensure it reflects the team's intent

Day 3 - Development

Goal Continue development
Team Daily stand-up
Devs Coding, testing and design
Product Owner Prepare for backlog grooming session by ranking stories and making sure stories are clear and ready for sizing by the team
Scrum Master Work with Product Owner to prepare for backlog grooming session

Day 4 - Backlog Grooming

Goal Continue development and groom backlog
Team Daily stand-up
Devs
  • Coding, testing and design
  • Meet with Product Owner to review, update and size backlog
Product Owner Backlog grooming session
Scrum Master Facilitate backlog grooming session

Day 5 - Development

Goal Continue development
Team Daily stand-up
Devs Coding, testing and design
Product Owner Accept completed stories
Scrum Master Review stories in backlog

Day 6 & 7 - Don’t Work!

Scrum Master Be Merry
